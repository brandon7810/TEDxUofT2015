TEDxUofT
2015 Conference

TEDxUofT will be holding its 3rd annual conference, bringing once again UofT's brightest minds and ideas together in the spirit of a TED-like experience.

‘A Constellation of Insights’ was chosen as the theme for TEDxUofT 2015. This theme allows for many different interpretations. A constellation represents both individuality and collaboration, illustrating how ‘connecting the dots’ can lead to fantastical discoveries; a concept which can be translated across fields and cultures. While each star can be easily distinguished from its neighbours, it is the collective formation that ultimately produces the picture humans have observed looking up at the heavens. With an ancient history in both creativity and practicality, constellations form the basis of communication; inspiring story-tellers and guiding travellers for thousands of years.

Joshua Liu, Healthcare

Dr. Joshua Liu is a physician turned entrepreneur who believes that the future of healthcare depends on the intersection of design, data and technology. He is currently the co-founder & CEO of SeamlessMD, a healthcare technology company advancing patient care in surgery using mobile & web technology. During his medical training, Joshua co-led a project at the University Health Network’s Centre for Innovation in Complex Care on hospital readmissions. Joshua was named a Forbes 30 Under 30 in Healthcare in 2014 and previously a Canadian Top 20 Under 20.

Samantha Joel

Samantha Joel takes a data-driven approach to the age-old question of what makes romantic relationships work. Decisions such as whether to pursue a particular love interest, whether to commit to a new dating partner, or whether to end a struggling relationship can have a profound influence on the path that our lives ultimately take. But how do people make these crucial choices, and how might we make better choices? Samantha Joel is currently exploring these research questions as a PhD student in the Psychology Department at the University of Toronto. By collecting data about real-world relationships using modern scientific methods, Samantha hopes to uncover useful decision strategies that will ultimately lead to happier, healthier relationships. Samantha has published 12 peer-reviewed articles on this topic in journals such as Psychological Science and Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin. Samantha is a regular contributor for Science of Relationships (www.scienceofrelationships.com) as well as for Psychology Today, with the goal of making research findings more accessible to the public.

Jeffrey Pilscher, Food Historian

Jeffrey Pilcher studies world history for answers to the dilemmas of health, sustainability, and justice within the global food system. What should we eat? Humans have struggled with this question throughout history. Advances in nutritional science and the rise of the industrial food system have seemingly overcome the periodic famines of the past, at least for those living in affluent societies. Yet paradoxically we have become ever more anxious about what we eat. Is it healthy for us and for our planet? Where is it produced, by whom, and why does it matter? Jeffrey Pilcher seeks to put these dilemmas in historical perspective by examining how people have fed themselves around the world in times past. He is the author of several books, including Planet Taco: A Global History of Mexican Food and Food in World History. He teaches classes on the world history of food and drink at the University of Toronto and is an affiliated faculty member of the Culinaria Research Project, whose mission is to document, preserve, and study the world’s culinary diversity.

Tobias Chen, Futurist

Tobias Chen explores the synergy between humans and wearables and demonstrates how the future of mediated reality systems can change the way we communicate and collaborate. Tobias Chen is a technologist and futurist born and raised in Vancouver, BC and currently studying Interactive Digital Media at the University of Toronto. He is a researcher at the Humanistic Intelligence Laboratory, working with Dr. Steve Mann and colleagues on creating both augmented and virtual reality systems through wearable computing and computer vision technologies.
